About Me

Hello! My name is Kodjo Tchinou. I am from Togo, a small country located in West Africa. I have 3 sisters and 2 little brothers. My father is a businessman, and my mother is also involved in business. I am passionate about technology and software development. Currently, I am learning Web Design Tools with the amazing professor Stephen Herd to build amazing websites, and I am excited about how technology can create positive changes in the world. In my spare time, I like to explore new programming languages and work on projects that can help me grow as a developer. Learning new skills every day is something I value deeply, as I believe it will open up more opportunities for me in the future.

Hobbies

When I am not coding, I enjoy playing musical instruments such as the piano and guitar. These hobbies allow me to express my creativity in different ways and offer a great balance to my tech-focused interests. Additionally, I enjoy reading books on a variety of topics, including technology, history, and self-improvement. I also play video games as a way to relax and challenge myself. Gaming has taught me the importance of teamwork and strategic thinking, which I believe is beneficial in both coding and everyday life.

My Goals

One of my main goals is to become a skilled full-stack developer. I want to continue learning more about web development and eventually build websites that have a meaningful impact. Through coding, I hope to contribute to the development of innovative solutions for the challenges facing my community. In the long term, I aim to create in my country Togo a tech company that will focus on Educational technology, providing accessible learning tools to people all over the world. I believe that technology can bridge gaps and make education more inclusive.
